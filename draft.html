This is essential because the data is less than 1% benign samples. Its not immediately apparent why this is a bad thing, but a classifier can always guess DDoS and achieve an over 99% accuracy. This is terrible. While we do not need an exact 50/50 split of data, we should experiment with different ways and ratios to split the data. This can drastically impact performance and it will also make it much easier to conduct experiments because we can perform them on a downsampled set of data. We can also use the large amount of left-over data to verify that our model performs correctly on unseen data 4. Do we want more false positives or false negatives? Which has the worst drawbacks from the perspective of network operators or users? With a DDoS attack, it is reasonable to let through some attacks because it is a massively distributed attack. The goal isnt to screen out 100% of the attacks, but just a high enough percentage to allow normal operation to continue. Similarly false positives that identify benign traffic as a DDoS attack could negatively impact a user just as severely as a DDoS attack. Screening users and forcing them to take “am i human” tests can drive away web traffic and be suboptimal defense strategies 6. Experiments 7. Results 8. Discussion 9.
